What's this all about?

Let me tell you. You're going to like this.

I believe Navigating Knowledge can help make education more efficient and equitable.

I have two long-term personal goals: to efficiently, and equitably, improve community health and education. Navigating Knowledge moves me closer to achieve the latter.

At the heart of Navigating Knowledge is the Navigating Knowledge Map. The map complements all existing educational materials on the market by organizing and visualizing content in a standardized viewing field. It is designed to utilize the area of our brain responsible for spacial relation, navigation, and memory.

I was also inspired by the hierarchy (or taxonomy) that American educational psychologist Benjamin Bloom advocated for in the 1950s. Building off of fundamental principles proposed by Bloom, the Navigating Knowledge Map clearly allows for educators to distinguish the levels of learning through visual layers on the map.

I think you will find the value of Navigating Knowledge. I feel this tool will improve educational attainment for all humans. I want you to use Navigating Knowledge.

Sincerely,

Dan Clark, founder and director of Navigating Knowledge
